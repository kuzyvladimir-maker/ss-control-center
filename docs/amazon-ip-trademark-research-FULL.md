## 1. The situation

Salutem Solutions sells **own-brand gift baskets / gift sets** (brands: *Salutem Vita*,
*Starfit*) that **contain genuine third-party-brand products** (e.g. Oscar Mayer franks,
Jimmy Dean sausage, Pepperidge Farm bread). We are NOT the rights holder of those third-
party brands. We have been receiving **Amazon intellectual-property (trademark)
complaints**, which can lead to listing removals and account blocks.

This document explains (a) why such gift baskets ARE permissible under the right
conditions, (b) the exact Amazon policy + US trademark doctrines that govern it, and
(c) what to do / not do — so an appeal can show the listings are compliant.

## 2. Amazon policy — the decisive facts (PRIMARY sourced)

- **Effective Oct 14, 2024 (enforced Jan 1, 2025), Amazon banned multi-brand consumables
  bundles — WITH an explicit "gift-basket exception."** Assortments suitable for gifting,
  in the gift-basket category, combining multiple brands, ARE permitted. *(Confirmed 3-0
  against the primary Amazon Product Bundling Policy PDF.)*
- A compliant gift basket must:
  1. Sit in the **gift-basket / gift category** with **cohesive gift presentation**
     (assembled as a gift, not a loose multi-pack).
  2. Have its **own UPC** (or an approved **GTIN exemption**) — not reuse a component's UPC.
  3. Contain **genuine items in their ORIGINAL, sealed manufacturer packaging**.
  4. Use a **title** that says **"Bundle" / "Gift Basket" + the item count**, and a **first
     bullet + description that factually identify the included products**.
  5. Use a **main image that shows the SPECIFIC ACTUAL products** included (no
     representative/stock stand-ins).
- **Myths that are FALSE (each refuted 3-0):** that the bundle must be branded by the
  highest-priced item; that the bundle must be sold by the manufacturer that owns all the
  brands; that self-assembling different manufacturers' products into your own gift basket
  is prohibited absent authorization. **The gift-basket exception specifically allows us to
  assemble and brand these baskets ourselves.**

## 3. US trademark law — why naming included brands is lawful (LEGAL sourced)

- **First-sale doctrine:** a buyer of genuine trademarked goods may resell them and use the
  mark to identify them, *provided* there is adequate disclosure, no consumer confusion,
  and **no "material difference"** from the original (improper repackaging/altering can void
  this). Doing *more than reselling* — or implying authorization/endorsement — loses the
  safe harbor. *(Confirmed 3-0.)*
- **Nominative fair use** (New Kids on the Block v. News America, 9th Cir. 1992; 3-factor
  test): you may **name** another's trademark to identify the genuine product. *(Confirmed
  3-0.)* **BUT using the brand's LOGO does not cleanly qualify** — the claim that logo use
  is protected nominative fair use was **REFUTED 0-3**. Name the brand in text; do not
  feature its logo as marketing/hero imagery.
- **Critical limit:** these doctrines bind COURTS, not Amazon's private takedown process.
  Amazon can remove a listing on a rights-holder complaint regardless of legal merit. The
  practical goal is to **give no one a reason to complain** — depict genuine product as-is,
  name brands only factually, never co-brand or imply a relationship.

## 4. DO / DON'T (compliant listing pattern)

**DO**
- Keep the listing in the gift-basket category with cohesive gift presentation; own UPC /
  GTIN exemption; genuine items in original sealed packaging.
- Title: "… Gift Basket / Bundle, N items"; bullet 1 + description factually list contents.
- Name third-party brands **only factually**: e.g. *"Includes 8 Oscar Mayer Bun Length
  Franks."*
- Include the curator disclaimer:
  - Bullet: *"Curated and assembled by Salutem Solutions LLC as a gift basket."*
  - Description: *"The included items are packaged by their original manufacturers."*
- Images: show the **actual included products in their original packaging**, assembled as
  the gift; the hero frames OUR basket under OUR brand.

**DON'T**
- ❌ Put a third-party brand in the **Brand field** or as the **lead/title brand**.
- ❌ Use **"authorized / official / endorsed / licensed / in partnership with."**
- ❌ Feature a third-party **LOGO** as the hero/focal point, in comparison charts, banners,
  or brand-story; no co-branding. (Don't blur logos either — show product as-is, but never
  make the logo the subject.)
- ❌ Repackage/alter the genuine products (material difference voids first-sale protection).
- ❌ Add long defensive/legal paragraphs, affiliation-negation ("not affiliated with…"),
  trademark-ownership statements, or supply-chain claims — these trigger Amazon's
  promotional/false-claims classifier (PDP code 99300) and read as claims, not disclaimers.
- ❌ Promotional adjectives, emojis, or health claims.

## 5. How this supports an IP-complaint response / reinstatement (not legal advice)

- Establish that the products are **genuine** (purchased from legitimate retail), sold in
  **original packaging** (no material difference) → **first-sale doctrine** applies.
- Show the listing uses the brand name **nominatively and factually** (to identify
  contents), with the **curator disclaimer**, and **does not use the rights-holder's logo
  as marketing imagery** or imply endorsement → no source confusion.
- Show the basket qualifies under Amazon's **gift-basket exception** to the Oct-2024
  bundling policy (gift category, own UPC, actual products shown).
- If a specific complaint cites logo/image use, remediate by removing any logo-forward
  imagery (keep product shown as-is) and re-submit.
