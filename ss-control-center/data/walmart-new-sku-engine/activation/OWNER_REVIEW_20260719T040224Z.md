## Targeted one-donor evidence lane

The source now contains a sealed `TARGETED_WALMART_EVIDENCE` lane for the single exact
candidate gap. Its two identity modes are `EXISTING_EXACT` and
`OWNER_ATTESTED_BOOTSTRAP`. Final focused and integration certification on the final
source bytes is still pending; this statement does not authorize execution. No
Oxylabs or Unwrangle call was made while implementing it.

An engine-generated doctor request binds exactly one existing `DonorProduct`, exactly
one direct first-party Walmart `DonorOffer`, the numeric Walmart item ID, normalized
product URL, DB/schema/migration/release fingerprints, and one canonical identity. For
a legacy donor, the engine first emits an owner-review/template and stops. The owner
proposes the canonical identity outside the Claude Code workspace. External owner
custody of an approval bound to the resulting exact targeted `plan.sha256` is the
bootstrap identity attestation; the plan by itself does not claim authenticated owner
authorship.

The run is bounded to one Oxylabs Walmart query (`1` unit), one Unwrangle Walmart detail
call (`2.5` units), ZIP `33765`, a 24-hour price TTL, at least two exact-variant images,
and a `180000 ms` wall clock. OFF, clubs, BJ's, fanout, automatic replay, publication,
delist, reprice, and purchase are disabled. Existing complete exact content may be
reused only with fresh price evidence and only before any detail receipt exists.

Bootstrap cannot write before a fresh exact search. Its transaction fence permits at
most one canonical variant and one decision for the sealed donor/offer, and rolls back
if the writer would create or touch an unrelated donor, offer, variant, or decision.
Claude Code may only execute the frozen doctor→plan→execute/resume/status/report CLI;
it may not code, use SQL, construct identity, or call a provider manually.

This is a later, separate per-target gate. It is not authorized by the Product Truth
migration approval requested below, and it is not the current next owner decision.
